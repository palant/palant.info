Geez, a pretty sad state of things if you ask me Great article!

I'd say that at this point, the CWS team doesn't really have a reason to deal with this - CWS is already entrenched in terms of extension marketplaces (amongst browsers) and has 100s of millions of users. Not only that - developers are in abundance, and most won't even know about this (count me as an example, I only found out by links to that chromium groups thread from 2023).

Those that do, and those that do something about it, will mostly care about how it affects their products, which makes sense. So in those cases (perhaps like Norton) Google might do something [for them].

Finally, in the end the extensions still engage users - which is CWS's bottom line, and unfortunately, users do have them installed. So if Chrome removes the extensions, that might upset affected users, and we're talking 10s of millions.

Though still, I can only comment as an observer; it'd be a little foolish to think the team/Google doesn't have the resources to address this.

Regardless, good stuff!
